Winter

Plants can be planted in the winter, depending on your location and climate. Here are some examples of plants that can be planted during the winter: Winter vegetables, Ornamental plants, Trees and shrubs, Bulbs, and Winter annuals.

Some vegetables can thrive in colder temperatures, such as broccoli, Brussels sprouts, cabbage, cauliflower, kale, and spinach. These can be planted in the late summer or early fall to mature during the winter.

Some ornamental plants like winter jasmine, holly, and winterberry can be planted in the winter months for a pop of color and texture during the colder months.

Deciduous trees and shrubs can be planted in the winter while they are dormant. This allows them to establish their root systems before spring.

Certain bulbs like daffodils, tulips, and crocuses can be planted in the fall and will bloom during the winter and spring months.

Plants like pansies, violas, and snapdragons can thrive in colder weather and add color to winter gardens.

Spring

Spring is a great time to plant a variety of plants. Here are some examples of plants that can be planted in the spring: Annuals, Perennials, Vegetables, Herbs, and Fruit trees and berry bushes.

Plants like petunias, marigolds, and impatiens can be planted in the spring and will provide vibrant color throughout the growing season.

Perennial plants like daisies, black-eyed Susans, and coneflowers can be planted in the spring and will bloom year after year.

Many vegetable plants can be planted in the spring, including tomatoes, peppers, beans, cucumbers, and squash.

Herbs like basil, thyme, and cilantro can be planted in the spring and will provide fresh flavors for cooking throughout the growing season.

Fruit trees like apple and pear, as well as berry bushes like blueberry and raspberry, can be planted in the spring for a bountiful harvest in the future.

Summer

Plants can be planted in the summer, although it can be a bit more challenging due to the high temperatures and increased need for watering. Here are some examples of plants that can be planted in the summer: Annuals, Vegetables, Herbs, Tropical plants, and Succulents and cacti.

Summer annuals like zinnias, cosmos, and sunflowers can be planted in the summer for vibrant color.

Many vegetables can be planted in the summer, such as beans, corn, cucumbers, peppers, and tomatoes. It's important to provide ample water to help them thrive in the heat.

Herbs like basil, rosemary, and oregano can be planted in the summer and will thrive with regular watering.

Plants that thrive in hot and humid climates, like hibiscus, bougainvillea, and elephant ear, can be planted in the summer for lush, tropical foliage.

Drought-tolerant plants like succulents and cacti can be planted in the summer and will thrive in the heat with minimal watering.

Autumn

Fall is a great time to plant a variety of plants. Here are some examples of plants that can be planted in the fall: Bulbs, Perennials, Trees and shrubs, Cool-season vegetables, and Ornamental grasses.

Fall is the perfect time to plant spring-blooming bulbs like tulips, daffodils, and crocuses.

Fall is a great time to plant perennials like asters, mums, and sedum. Planting in the fall allows the roots to become established before winter and ensures they will bloom the following year.

Fall is an ideal time to plant trees and shrubs while the soil is still warm and the weather is mild. This allows the roots to become established before winter.

Vegetables that thrive in cooler temperatures, such as broccoli, Brussels sprouts, carrots, lettuce, and spinach, can be planted in the fall for a fall harvest.

Fall is an excellent time to plant ornamental grasses like fountain grass, maiden grass, and switchgrass for year-round interest.
